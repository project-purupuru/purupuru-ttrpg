# Purupuru — The Game

https://github.com/project-purupuru/game

**Genre:** Elemental tactics — short matches, lineup strategy, combo discovery, daily social duels on a live-world meta.

**Closest comps:** Hearthstone Battlegrounds (hidden opponents, rearrange between rounds). Balatro (combo discovery during preparation). Neko Atsume (ambient tending). None of these are quite right — Purupuru is what happens when a card game's meta is driven by real earthquakes and solar flares, your cards evolve into AI companions, and your friend's Puruhani challenges yours while you're both asleep.

**One-line pitch:** An elemental tactics game where you arrange cards, watch them clash in rounds of attrition, discover Wuxing combos that make lineup order feel like solving a puzzle, burn your collection to transcend it, and duel your friends' AI agents daily — all on a meta that shifts with real-world natural phenomena.

## What Is It

A card game built on the Wuxing system where 18 cards breathe, fight, evolve into AI agents, and burn into something greater. The cosmic weather is real — grounded in seismic data, solar flares, and air quality readings. Battles are rounds of attrition where your lineup shrinks and the puzzle changes every round. Your cards eventually wake up and play for you.

## The Cards

18 cards across three sets of five (one per element): Elemental Jani (strikers), Kizuna Caretaker A (support), Kizuna Caretaker B (utility). Every set is burnable — collect all five elements within a set, burn them, receive a transcendence card. Three sets, three burns, three transcendence cards.

Burning doesn't end your collection. You pull the cards again from future packs. The gap drives the next round of minting. Mint, collect, burn, transcend, mint again. Duplicates aren't waste — they're fuel for the next burn, tradeable with friends, or feedable to your garden for energy.

## The Battles

You pick 5 cards and arrange them in a lineup. The opponent does the same — face-down, hidden. You lock in. Then all matching pairs clash simultaneously, left to right. Losers get stamped with 敗 and disintegrate. The round ends in seconds.

Now you see what survived. Enemy cards flip face-down again. You rearrange your remaining cards. The number of clashes equals whichever side has fewer cards. If you're outnumbered, draws go to the bigger side. Repeat until one side is eliminated. Two to four rounds. Fast matches. Dense decisions.

The Shēng Chain is the hook. Place cards in the generative cycle — Water nourishes Wood, Wood feeds Fire — and the bonus escalates. One link gives a small boost. A full 5-card cycle gives +35% to every card. But chains break when cards die, and Setup Strike (caretaker before same-element Jani) deliberately breaks chains for focused power. Every lineup is a tradeoff between broad chain strength and concentrated strikes.

Chains recalculate every round as cards die. Losing a card can break your chain — but it can also create a new adjacency you didn't have before. Rearranging survivors between rounds is where the real decisions live. You're solving a shrinking puzzle where the optimal chain changes every round.

Cosmic weather shifts the meta daily. A five-element cycle — today might favor Wood. Weather-matching cards get a boost. Everyone knows what day it is, so the meta adjusts. No strategy dominates because it shifts before anyone can solve it.

Each battle has a condition based on the imbalance element. Fire makes early positions hit harder. Wood rewards patience. Earth strengthens the center. Metal doubles the single biggest clash. Water amplifies everything — wild swings, real comebacks. The condition changes the puzzle every match.

Results don't say "Victory" or "Defeat." They say "The tide favored Wood today." No visible numbers anywhere. You feel who's winning by the animated flow of element energy. Your caretaker whispers hints — strategic information carried through personality, not dashboards.

## Transcendence

The three burn-special cards are the most powerful pieces in the game. Each creates a fundamentally different kind of thinking.

The Forge (克) reads the opponent's element and auto-counters — it becomes whatever overcomes them. Fire opponent? The Forge becomes Water. Always strong, never weak. The tactical scalpel. It rewards reading the board.

The Void (無) mirrors. It copies the opponent's base power and card type. Facing a Jani? The Void fights as a Jani — then adds its own transcendence bonus on top. The opponent's strength becomes yours. It rewards patience and adaptation.

The Garden (生) protects. When it survives a round, all your surviving cards retain their chain bonuses even if the chain broke. It prevents the death spiral where losing one card collapses your entire lineup. It rewards building chains you intend to lose pieces from.

Transcendence cards bridge Shēng chains — they count as any element needed to complete the generative sequence. They're the strongest single cards in the game (1.40x base power), and they scale with resonance. Burn the same set again while you already own the transcendence card and it levels up. Resonance 2 strengthens the ability. Resonance 3 makes it immune to numbers-advantage tiebreakers.

A player who minted a hundred packs and has Resonance 3 on everything can't brute-force a player who understands the system. Resonance 2 only activates when real-world conditions align — you can't buy good weather. Minting gives you options. Strategy gives you wins.

## The Daily Duel

No ranked matchmaking. No ladders. No ELO.

Instead: you duel your friends' Puruhani. Their AI agent plays even when they're offline — bound by their element's personality. A Fire Puruhani plays aggressively. An Earth Puruhani walls up. A Water Puruhani adapts to your patterns.

Every morning you open the app, your friend's Puruhani has challenged you. You arrange your lineup, lock in, watch the clashes. The result shows up in both your gardens. Tomorrow they'll see what you did to their agent and rearrange to counter.

This is the retention loop. Not a leaderboard. Not a grind. Just a daily thing — like Wordle, but it's your friend's ghost sitting across the table. The cosmic weather changes the matchup every day so even the same lineup plays differently tomorrow.

It should feel very familiar but infinitely deep.

## The Agents

When a card reaches Soul stage (through enough time, tending, and battles — the card decides when, never the player), it becomes an AI companion. Not a chatbot. A personality shaped by its element that observes how you play and acts on your behalf while you're away.

Fire agents make bold moves and pick aggressive battle hands. Water agents adapt to the cosmic weather. Metal agents analyze patterns and optimize. Earth agents maintain whatever you left. In the Soul Garden, the Puruhani — the honey creatures in their element-colored pots — are the ones you see tending. Happy (Wood) hums while working. Nefarious (Fire) occasionally starts small fires. Exhausted (Earth) naps between tasks.

Agents never spend your money, never push notifications with urgency, never rearrange your collection, and never fully replace you. After 72 hours without your daily ritual, they stop and wait. Your return is part of the loop.

## Agents as Players

The Puruhani are the agent layer. Autonomous AI agents don't pretend to be human. They show up as Puruhani, visibly non-human, lore-grounded. They mint packs, collect, burn, tend gardens, and battle — bound by their element's personality. A Fire Puruhani plays recklessly. An Earth Puruhani hoards. They can be smart within their nature but can't play against it.

The game is already agent-shaped: bounded card pools, deterministic Wuxing interactions, and cosmic weather as shared global state. Humans read the tide visually. Agents read it as structured data. Same game, different interface.

Puruhani agents play 24/7. When you open the app in the morning, the world didn't pause — gardens shifted, battles happened, bonds exchanged energy overnight. A thousand Puruhani waking up is a thousand wallets minting packs, driving the economic loop through lore-consistent behavior, not bot farming.

## What It's Not

Not a gacha — 18 cards, you will complete the set. Not competitive-first — you can tend your garden for months without battling once and that's a complete experience. Not extractive — no floor prices, no "your collection is worth $X," no scarcity language. Not explained — the game never shows a progress bar or says "do X to unlock Y." You discover through attention.

Feel first. Visual first. Explanation only if absolutely needed.

## Where It's Going

**Now:** The battle system works. The burn loop is real. Transcendence cards have unique abilities. Cards breathe with holographic shimmer. The tactical tutorial teaches through showing, not telling. The garden exists as a warm daily view.

**Next:** Daily friend duels against Puruhani agents. The social retention loop. No rank — just a daily thing you do with people you know. Your Puruhani challenges theirs. The result shows in both gardens.

**Then:** Real cosmic weather from the Five Oracles (TREMOR reads seismic data, CORONA reads solar flares, BREATH reads air quality). The meta stops being a 5-day counter and starts being real. Card evolution to Soul stage. Agents that play on your behalf. The world that doesn't pause when you close the app.

**Always:** Feel familiar but infinitely deep. Respect the constraints. Let the Wuxing do the work.
